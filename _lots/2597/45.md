TYPESET: Lithograph on ivory laid paper, 1896.  172x127 mm; 6¾x5 inches, full margins.  Edition
  of approximately 28.  A very good impression of this scarce lithograph with all
  the details distinct.<br><br><i>Waterloo Bridge</i> is one of 8 lithographs drawn
  by Whistler (1834-1903) while he and his wife Beatrix resided at the Savoy Hotel
  in London in February and March 1896.  Beatrix had been diagnosed with cancer in
  1894 and was ill through 1895, with her condition worsening during the early months
  of 1896.  She died on May 10th, 1896.  Whistler's years of marriage with Beatrix,
  whom he nicknamed "Trixie," were among the happiest of his life.  He was devasted
  with her passing.<br><br>Their rooms at the Savoy Hotel were on the top floor, which
  afforded Whistler views of the Embankment and the Thames.  He spent much of his
  time during these painful months at his wife's bedside and drawing from the balconies
  of their suite.<br><br>With—<i>Old Battersea Bridge</i>, lithograph, 1879. 144x332
  mm; 5¾x13 inches, full margins.  Second state (of 2).  Edition of 100.  Published
