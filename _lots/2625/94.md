His work appeared in a variety of publications including “Atlantic Monthly,” “Cosmopolitan,” “Ladies Home Journal,” “McCall’s,” “The New York Times,” “The Saturday Evening Post,” and “The Washington Post.” In addition to those, he worked for publishers such as Ballantine, Berkley, Doubleday, Putnam, HBJ, Macmillan and Time Life, among many others. Tinkelman was commissioned by the National Park Service to do drawings and paintings of national parks and monuments, as well as by the U.S. Air Force to be an artist-reporter on specific missions. His shows included a one-man exhibit of his baseball art at The National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum in Cooperstown, New York in 1994, and The United States Sports Academy in Daphne, Alabama in 1995."
