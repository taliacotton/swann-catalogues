TYPESET: 'Pen and ink and pencil on cream wove paper, double-sided, circa 1930.  267x180
  mm; 10½x7⅛ inches.  Signed in pencil, lower left verso.  <br><br>Provenance: Private
  collection, New York.<br><br>Bishop (1902-1988) was born in Cincinnati, though moved
  often as a child. She began studying art at age 12 in Detroit at the John Wicker
  Art School. In 1918, she moved to New York and continued her studies at the New
  York School of Applied Design for Women and the Art Students League where she studied
  with Kenneth Hayes Miller who greatly influenced her.<br><br>She acquired her studio
  in Union Square in 1926 and become part of a group of artists known as the Fourteenth
  Street School who depicted the urban environment and its inhabitants in a realist
  manner. She often depicted the New Woman that first emerged in the 1920s who worked
  outside the home; her subjects were often shopping, riding the subway and enjoying
  urban life. She looked towards Old Masters, particularly Dutch and Flemish artists,
  in how she rendered the form and movement of city dwellers.'
