During World War I she worked as a combat artist on the European front depicting soldiers and French refugees.  She is known primarily as a portraitist who depicted Philadelphia socialites, but she also painted and etched landscapes and seascapes.  Many of those works depict the scenery on Monhegan Island in Maine, where she rented and eventually bought her cousin Rockwell Kent's (1882-1971, see l<a href=https://catalogue.swanngalleries.com/auction-catalogs/19TH--20TH-CENTURY-ART?saleno=2560&viewby=Lot_asc&filter_value=Rockwell%20Kent>lots 142-146</a>) cottage and studio."
