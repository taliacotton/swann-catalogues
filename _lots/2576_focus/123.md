(3)<br><br>Hays Hammond collaborated with Martha Graham and Alice Laughlin to produce \"Six Miracle Plays\" at the Guild Theater in 1933. One may visit her Hammond Museum & Japanese Stroll Garden, chartered in 1957 in North Salem, New York.<br><br>Sardeau was a founding member of the Sculptors Guild. Her work includes <i>The Slave</i>, at the Ellen Phillips Samuel Memorial in Philadelphia, <i>Mother and Child</i>, at the Whitney, and the bronze, <i>Kneeling Woman</i>, at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Her work focused on compassion and violence. In this portrait, she poses with her infant son, and husband George Biddle.<br><br>Wright was an accomplished painter and writer with interests in philanthropy and the conservation of nature.
