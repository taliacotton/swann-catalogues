TYPESET: <p><em>Cocorico</em> was a magazine published between 1898 and 1902. As a
  literary and artistic publication, its main role was to promote the Art Nouveau
  movement and its primary artists included Mucha, Steinlen, de Feure, Villon and
  many others. In all, there were 63 issues. The name of the periodical is the French
  onomatopoeia for "cock-a-doodle-doo," the crowing of a rooster. In this instance,
  Steinlen employs the title as an exceptional wake-up call for the world to notice
  this little magazine and the big ideas contained within.</p><p>This is the larger
