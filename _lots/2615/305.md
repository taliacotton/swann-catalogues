FOOTNOTE: "On Armistice Day in 1919, an American Legion parade in Centralia, WA stopped in front of the union hall of the Industrial Workers of the World (the Wobblies), a year after the hall was looted during another parade. Soon five legionnaires and one of the Wobblies were dead, with the facts and meaning of these events still hotly debated to this day. Offered here are 24 items on the tragedy issued from 1919 to 1935, and 24 additional books and ephemera published more recently. Many but not all of these items are cited in Miles, \"Something in Common--An IWW Bibliography.\" Period material in this lot includes: <br><br>1. \"You Ought to Know\", 4 pages on one folding sheet.
