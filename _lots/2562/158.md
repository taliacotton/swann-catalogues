FOOTNOTE: "These photographs depict one of the first of the lunch counter sit-ins which swept the United States in the early 1960s. 7 young members of the Congress on Racial Equality (5 Black, 2 White) sat at the Woolworth's counter in the French Quarter of New Orleans. The staff refused to serve them; they refused to leave. The stand-off ended almost 5 hours later with their arrest. These photographs are the original prints from the files of the New Orleans Times-Picayune, taken by their staff photographer Ralph Uribe."
