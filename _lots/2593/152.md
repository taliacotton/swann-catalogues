McCulloch 80.<br><br>According to McCulloch, author of the catalogue <i>raisonné</i> of Freeman's lithographs, the scene is, \"At a barbershop/polling place on Clinton Street near Williamsburg Bridge, business was not suspended on election day.  In this election a powerful political machine was defeated, and Fiorello La Guardia became the mayor of New York City.  A policeman told Freeman, 'The people is gettin' educated.'\"<br><br>Freeman was born in San Diego, California and studied at the San Diego School of Fine Arts. He moved to New York in 1929, just days before the stock market crashed, and continued his studies at the Art Students League. He developed a social realist style, often depicting life around Times Square. During the 1930s he worked in the graphics division of the WPA. Later in his career he illlustrated and wrote childrens' book, most famously <i>Corduroy</i>."
