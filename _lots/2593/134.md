FOOTNOTE: "Fort Peck was a major project of the Public Works Administration (PWA), itself part of the New Deal. Construction of Fort Peck Dam started in 1933, and at its peak in July 1936 employed 10,546 workers. Assigned by Henry Luce to cover the massive New Deal project, Bourke-White photographed the dam, the spillway, and daily life in the surrounding boomtown. Her image <i>Fort Peck Dam, Montana</i> was published on the cover of the inaugural issue of <i>LIFE</i> magazine on November 23, 1936."
