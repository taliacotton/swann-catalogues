Helmont performed an experiment on a willow tree, whose results were published in this work for the first time. In it, he measured the initial weight of a young tree and its soil, and then continued to weigh all of the water he gave the plant. After five years, the tree had gained 164 pounds, although the weight of the soil remained the same.
