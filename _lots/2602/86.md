He worked first in Rome, and was later appointed to the Florentine court, where he made etchings associated with the Medici festivals and theatrical productions.  He returned to Nancy in 1621, renowned as a printmaker of innovative technique. His  oeuvre  consists of more than 1,400 prints.  Callot returned to this ambitious, cataclysmic subject, which he had treated first in 1617, just before his death.
