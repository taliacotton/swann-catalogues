Spreter, an artist, portrait painter, illustrator, was born in Chicago in 1899. By the time hewas just 20 years old he had become a commercial artist at Grauman & Company in Chicago.There he was a member of the Palette & Chisel Club where he participated in a 1922 exhibition.In the early 1920s he moved to the Philadelphia, PA area and maintained his home and studio inthe suburb of Gladwyne, PA from 1926. He also maintained a studio in New York City. In 1928 heparticipated in the exhibition “Seven Men” at the Philadelphia Art Club. Spreter was a memberof the Philadelphia Art Alliance and The Art Directors Club of New York.Spreter was an artist, illustrator and portrait painter. His commercial clients included Cream ofWheat, Look Magazine, Women’s Home Companion, The American Magazine, Ladies’ HomeJournal, Liberty magazine, Standard Sanitary Mfg., and others. He also was well-regarded as aportrait painter, including the 1934 cover portrait of Dana Suesse for “The Night is Young:Concert Music by Dana”.Spreter’s studio was designed by the renowned architect, William Lescaze (of the famous firstinternational modern skyscraper, 1932’s PSFS Building, in Philadelphia."
