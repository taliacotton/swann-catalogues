Michael Deskey, 18 Nov 2006.\"<br>Witold Gordon was a Polish-born American Art Deco painter best known for his murals in Radio City Music Hall completed in 1932. He painted a 6,000 square foot mural for the 1939 New York World's Fair and a series of New Yorker covers depicting the city in its recent past. He also designed the poster for the original Broadway production of Rodgers and Hammerstein's Oklahoma! (1943).<br>Prominent American designer, Donald Deskey, coordinated the painting of murals at Radio City Music Hall and commissioned Gordon, among the period's foremost avant-garde artists, along with Stuart Davis and William Zorach, to create two of them.
